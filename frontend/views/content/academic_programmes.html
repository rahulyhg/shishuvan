Early Years Curriculum

Our early years curriculum comprises the following classes: Nursery – Junior Kindergarten – Senior Kindergarten.

The Early Years Program (EYP) consists of the first years of school life (ages 3 to 5). In the EYP, we aim to provide a safe and stimulating environment in which children can develop socially, emotionally, creatively, physically and academically and provide a broad, balanced an relevant curriculum.

In Early Years our academic programme is based on developmentally appropriate practice with an emphasis on your child’s social-emotional development and wellbeing.

We begin preparing children for structured daily routines by facilitating activities that allow for logical reasoning, critical thinking and problem-solving strategies. Activities are based around themes and topics as well as children’s interests and needs. All areas of the curriculum are supported through large and small group sessions with both planned and spontaneous child-initiated activities. Through indoor and outdoor play, children become confident in their own learning, are able to take risks, are effective communicators and can articulate their thoughts, feelings and ideas to others. Children are exposed to phonics, reading and writing simple words. To understand a growing range of simple mathematical concepts, children are exposed to recalling number symbols, shapes and patterns.

The Importance of Play

Play is the highest form of research

-Albert Einstein

Through play, your child will explore their immediate environment and begin to make sense of the world around them. There are opportunities for indoor and outdoor learning, engagement in sensory exploration activities and opportunities to develop creative and motor skills in different ways. There are role-play opportunities for children to develop their communication skills and story-times where language will be continually extended. The language-rich environment provides multiple experiences for each child’s verbal and non-verbal communication skills to grow.

Primary School Curriculum

The Primary School comprises Grades 1 to 5. In this section, we plan to offer the Cambridge Primary Curriculum. A wide range of subjects including Mathematics, English, Hindi, Marathi, Science, Humanities, ICT (Computers), Visual Arts, Music and Physical Education will be offered in this section. Our children follow inquiry-based units focused on; looking after the environment; internationalism; entrepreneurial understanding; and a reflection on the past. Each year children will take part in a community project as we aim to prepare them for life as responsible global citizens and foster the desire to contribute to their local communities. Our school environment encourages and empowers young students to ask questions, safe to meet challenges head on, and accompanied on their journey towards reaching their full potential. This is a time in which to instill a love of learning that children will carry with them throughout their lives. Classes are small, taught by expert staff making personalised learning a reality. Our teaching pedagogy ensures that students actively participate and engage in lessons, and develop personally as well as academically. Success is celebrated, and students emerge as confident, curious and enthusiastic learners.
